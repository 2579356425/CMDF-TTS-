CMDF-TTS：Rapid and high-quality text-to-speech method with limited target speaker data

Abstract

While end-to-end text-to-speech (TTS) methods with limited target speaker corpus can generate high-quality speech, they often require a substantial amount of non-target speaker corpus (auxiliary corpus) to train the TTS model, leading to a significant increase in training costs. In this work, we propose a novel, efficient approach for speech synthesis with a limited target speaker corpus, which reduces the costs of training, requiring few target speaker recordings. This strategy enables expedited model training, reducing the cost of speech synthesis. Initially, we implement corpus compression on an auxiliary corpus, serving to reduce the requisite training time. Subsequently, we train the proposed non-autoregressive model CMDF-TTS using the compressed auxiliary corpus. To mitigate quality degradation in the synthesized speech, resulting from the corpus compression, we integrate multi-level prosody modeling and use denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs) as decoders for generating mel-spectrograms. Furthermore, to incorporate the characteristics of the target speaker into the model, we perform fine-tuning using the target speaker corpus. Finally, we employ conditional variational auto-encoder generative adversarial networks (CVAE-GAN) for additional fine-tuning, further enhancing the naturalness of the synthesized speech. We validate the effectiveness of our proposed method through extensive experiments on Mandarin and English corpora. The results indicate that our approach effectively balances model training speed with synthesized speech quality, outperforming current state-of-the-art models.
